Essential Travel Tips

Make the most of your trip by knowing what to expect and how to behave. Our tips cover entry rules, money matters, etiquette and more.

Entry & Visa

Digital Arrival Card: From 1 May 2025 all non‑Thai nationals must submit an online Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) within 72 hours of arrival. It replaces the paper TM6 form and requires details of your journey, accommodation and a health declaration. You can register multiple travellers (up to 10 people) in one application.

Visa Exemptions: Citizens from many countries including the UK and Ireland can stay visa‑free for up to 60 days. Travellers from other regions may need to apply for a visa or use visa on arrival. Always check official rules before your trip.

Insurance & Health: Healthcare in Thailand is not free for visitors. Purchase comprehensive travel insurance covering medical care and emergency evacuation. Pack any prescription medications in original containers and carry insect repellent to guard against mosquitoes.

Money Matters

Currency: The Thai baht (THB) is the only legal currency. Although some vendors quote prices in US dollars, they offer unfavourable exchange rates. Bring a mix of cash and cards. Cash is essential for markets, street food and rural areas where credit cards aren’t accepted. Use ATMs or currency booths to withdraw baht.

Card Use: When using a card, opt to pay in baht to avoid dynamic currency conversion fees. Notify your bank before travelling to prevent card blocks, and carry spare cash as backup.

Etiquette & Respect

Dress Modestly: When visiting temples or royal sites, cover shoulders and knees. Remove your shoes before entering worship halls and never point your feet at a Buddha statue. Women should avoid hugging or touching monks.

Mindful Behaviour: Keep voices low and avoid public displays of affection. To greet someone, press your palms together in a wai and bow your head slightly. Return smiles— Thailand is nicknamed the “Land of Smiles” for a reason.

Do’s & Don’ts: Don’t step on thresholds when entering homes or temples, don’t touch people’s heads, and don’t raise your voice in frustration. Vaping and e‑cigarettes are illegal, and smoking is banned on many beaches. Respect the monarchy: stand for the national anthem when it plays in public places such as cinemas.

Weather & When to Visit

The best time to visit is during the cool, dry season from November through early April when temperatures range between 29–36°C (84–97°F). In these months the south‑west coast has calm seas ideal for diving and snorkelling. From May to October expect hot, humid weather and short but intense downpours; this is low season so crowds thin out and prices drop.

Responsible Tourism

Support local communities by choosing ethically run tours and activities. Avoid riding elephants or posing with captive wildlife. Respect nature by disposing of waste properly and conserving water. Shop locally and avoid bargaining aggressively – the difference in price is small to you but meaningful to vendors.
