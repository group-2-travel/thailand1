Fascinating Facts

Delight in surprising insights about Thailand’s wildlife, history and traditions.

Did You Know?

Never Colonised

Thailand (formerly Siam) is the only nation in Southeast Asia that was never colonised by a European power. It maintained independence through diplomatic skill and strategic alliances.

Smallest Mammal

The bumblebee bat, native to western Thailand, weighs just two grams and is considered the world’s smallest mammal.

Largest Fish

The coastal waters host whale sharks – the largest fish on Earth. These gentle giants can grow up to 12 metres long.

Longest City Name

Bangkok’s ceremonial name has 168 letters! Translated from Thai, it extols the ‘great city of angels, repository of divine gems’ and more – making it one of the world’s longest place names.

Thousands of Temples

There are over 35,000 temples dotted across Thailand, ranging from ornate royal pagodas to humble forest hermitages. Respectful attire and conduct are required when visiting.

National Flower: Ratchaphruek

In October 2001 Thailand designated the ratchaphruek (Cassia fistula) – also known as the golden shower tree – as its national flower. Its clusters of bright yellow blossoms symbolise Thai royalty and good fortune. Orchids remain popular exports and symbols of grace but they are not the official national flower.
