Why Visit Thailand?

Uncolonised Heritage

Unlike its neighbours, Thailand was never colonised by a European power, preserving a distinct culture and national identity.

Festivals & Seasons

The cool and dry months from November to early April are perfect for travel. Don’t miss the mid‑April Songkran festival when water‑splashed celebrations welcome the Thai New Year.

Flavours & Adventure

Thai cuisine blends sweet, sour, salty and spicy in dishes like khao soi and som tum. Pair your meals with adventures on islands, in rainforests or amongst mountain tribes.

Plan Ahead

Entry Requirements

From 1 May 2025 travellers must complete a Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) online within 72 hours before arriving in Thailand. Visa exemptions allow stays up to 60 days for citizens of many countries, but check eligibility before departure. It’s also essential to purchase travel insurance as you’re liable for medical costs.

Currency & Etiquette

The Thai baht (THB) is the only legal tender. Carry cash for small purchases and avoid being charged in other currencies. When visiting temples, cover shoulders and knees, remove your shoes and behave respectfully – a smile goes a long way.
