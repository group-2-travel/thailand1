Must‑Visit Destinations

From vibrant cityscapes to serene beaches and historic ruins, explore the diverse regions that make Thailand unforgettable.

Bangkok

Thailand’s capital is a bustling metropolis where ancient temples stand beside glittering malls. Cruise along the Chao Phraya River to see the spires of Wat Arun and the Emerald Buddha at the Grand Palace. Explore floating markets, sample street food at night bazaars and wander through Chinatown’s gold shops and tea houses. Bangkok is also a gateway for day trips to nearby attractions such as the Damnoen Saduak floating market and the historic city of Ayutthaya.

Chiang Mai

Laid‑back Chiang Mai nestles amid forested mountains in northern Thailand. Wander through the walled Old City to discover Lanna‑style temples, then hike up Doi Suthep to admire the gold‑topped Wat Phra That overlooking the city. Chiang Mai is famous for its arts, coffee shops and markets. Visit during Songkran for a city‑wide water fight or Yi Peng to release paper lanterns into the night sky. Don’t leave without tasting the signature dish, khao soi, a creamy curry noodle soup topped with crispy fried noodles.

Phuket & Krabi

Phuket is Thailand’s largest island and a gateway to the Andaman Sea. Beyond the lively Patong beach you’ll find secluded bays, mangrove forests and a coastline dotted with limestone cliffs. Island hop to Phi Phi or hike to viewpoints for panoramic sunsets. Nearby Krabi province offers equally spectacular scenery with sheer karst towers rising from turquoise lagoons. Kayak through hidden caves at Ao Thalane, rock‑climb at Railay or relax on the white sands of Koh Poda.

Ayutthaya

Once the thriving capital of a mighty Siamese kingdom, Ayutthaya’s ruins now rest among tranquil gardens and moats. Towering prang (Khmer‑style stupas) and headless Buddha statues evoke the grandeur of its 14th‑to‑18th century heyday. Cycle through the Historical Park, visit Wat Mahathat where tree roots encircle a Buddha’s head, and take a sunset boat ride around the island to witness the golden glow of crumbling temples reflected in the river.
