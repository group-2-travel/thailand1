Culture & Cuisine

Thailand’s vibrant festivals and flavourful dishes reflect a rich heritage and a zest for life. Immerse yourself in traditions and taste the difference.

Songkran – Thai New Year

Celebrated each April when the sun moves into the Aries constellation, Songkran marks the traditional Thai New Year. Families reunite, pay respect to elders and visit temples to pour scented water over Buddha images and monks. Sprinkling and splashing water on one another symbolises washing away misfortune and bringing prosperity. Streets transform into joyful water fights with music and dancing. Join the fun responsibly by using clean water and protecting your belongings from getting drenched.

Signature Dishes

Khao Soi

This northern speciality is a creamy curry noodle soup made from coconut milk and spices, topped with crispy egg noodles. It’s served with pickled mustard greens, diced shallots and lime for a tangy counterbalance.

Som Tum

This refreshing salad combines shredded green papaya, crushed peanuts, garlic, chilli and dried shrimp tossed in a sauce of lime juice, fish sauce and palm sugar. Each bite balances sweet, sour, salty and spicy flavours.

Pad Thai

Thailand’s famous stir‑fried noodle dish features rice noodles tossed with tamarind sauce, tofu, prawns, bean sprouts and peanuts. Squeeze a wedge of lime over the top and adjust the chilli to your taste.

Cultural Highlights

Thailand’s artistic heritage flourishes in traditional dance, silk weaving and the intricate craftsmanship of temples and royal palaces. Classical Thai dance blends graceful movements and ornate costumes to depict stories from Buddhist and Hindu epics. Hand‑woven silk from Isaan and Chiang Mai is coveted worldwide. At markets you’ll find elaborately carved fruit displays and delicate flower garlands used as offerings. Participate in a workshop to learn the art of Thai massage or Muay Thai boxing – both rooted in ancient practices.
